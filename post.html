GST Council Meeting Update

Updates from 43rd GST Council meeting held on 28 May 2021. I have summarized few relevant recommendations made during the meeting below for your ready reference.

Posted by Money Space on May 30, 2021

1. Amnesty Scheme to provide relief to taxpayers regarding late fees for pending returns

To cap late fee for non-furnishing of Form GSTR 3B for tax periods from July 2017 to April 2021 to INR 500 (INR 250 each for CGST and SGST) per return for taxpayers who did not have any tax liability for the said tax period and INR 1,000 (INR 500 each for CGST and SGST) for other taxpayers. Such reduced rate of late fee would apply if the return for these tax periods is furnished between 1 June 2021 to 31 August 2021.

2. Rationalization of late fee

To reduce burden of late fee on taxpayers for delay in filing Form GSTR 1 and Form GSTR 3B, the upper cap of late fee to be rationalized as per below table :

Nil tax liability INR 500 (INR 250 each for CGST and SGST)
Taxpayers having Annual Aggregate Turnover (AATO) upto INR 1.5 Crores INR 2,000 (INR 1,000 each for CGST and SGST)
Taxpayers having AATO between INR 1.5 Crores to INR 5 Crores INR 5,000 (INR 2,500 each for CGST and SGST)
Taxpayers having AATO above INR 5 Crores INR 10,000 (INR 5,000 each for CGST and SGST)

Further late fee payable for delayed filing of Form GSTR 7 to be reduced to INR 50 per day (INR 25 each for CGST and SGST) and capped to maximum of INR 2,000 (INR 1,000 each for CGST and SGST) per return.

3. Further relaxations to taxpayers in addition to relaxations already provided vide notification dated 1 May 2021

For Small taxpayers (aggregate turnover upto INR 5 crores) I. Tax period March 2021 and April 2021 Nil rate of interest for first 15 days from due date of furnishing Form GSTR 3B or filing of PMT- 06 challan, reduced rate of 9% thereafter for 45 days and 30 days for March 2021 and April 2021 respectively. Waiver of late fee for delay in filing Form GSTR 3B for March 2021 and April 2021 for 60 days and 45 days respectively from due date. II. Tax period May 2021 Nil rate of interest for first 15 days from due date of furnishing Form GSTR 3B or filing of PMT- 06 challan, reduced rate of 9% thereafter for 15 days. Waiver of late fee for delay in filing Form GSTR 3B for May 2021 for 30 days from due date. For Large taxpayers (aggregate turnover above INR 5 crores) Lower rate of interest @ 9% for first 15 days after due date for filing Form GSTR 3B for tax period May 2021. Waiver of late fee for delay in filing Form GSTR 3B for the period May 2021 for 15 days from due date. 4. 4. Other relaxations Extension of due date of filing Form GSTR 1/IFF for the month of May 2021 by 15 days and extension in due date of filing Form ITC-04 for quarter ended March 2021 to 30 June 2021. Cumulative application of Rule 36(4) for availing ITC for tax periods April, May and June 2021 in the return for the period June 2021. Allowing filing of returns by companies using EVC till 31 August 2021. Time limit for completion of various actions, by any authority or by any person, under the GST Act, which falls during the period 15 April 2021 to 29 June 2021 to be extended to 30 June 2021. Amendments in relation to annual return and reconciliation statement (audit report) made through Finance Act to be notified. Such changes to apply for the period FY 2020-21. Further filing of annual return to be made optional for taxpayers having AATO upto 2 crores and reconciliation statement by taxpayers with AATO above 5 crores. Retrospective amendment in Section 50 of the CGST Act, 2017 w.e.f. 1 July 2017, providing for payment of interest on net cash basis, to be notified at the earliest. Exemption from IGST on import of certain covid-19 specific relief goods upto 31 August 2021.
